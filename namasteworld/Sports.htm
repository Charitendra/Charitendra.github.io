Sports:

 

  • The chess is originated in Bharat during the Gupta dynasty (c. 280-550 CE) known as chatrang and shatranj respectively.

 

  • “Snakes and ladders” (Saanp sidhi) originated in Bharat as a game based on morality. This game made its way to England, and was eventually introduced in the United States of America by game-pioneer Milton Bradley in 1943.

 

  • Ludo (Pachisi) originated in Bharat by the 6th century. The earliest evidence of this game in Bharat is the depiction of boards on the caves of Ajanta. This game made its way to England during the British Raj.

 

  • The game of kabaddi originated in Bharat during prehistory. Most of the authorities agree that the game existed in some form or the other in Bharat during the period between 1500-400 BCE.

 

  • Badminton: The modern form of Badminton can be traced to Bharat. This game was locally known as "pūppanatu viḷaiyāţţam" (Tamil), literally meaning "flower-ball game".
  • Cockfighting was a pastime in the Indus Valley Civilization by 2000 BC. The sport was popular in ancient times in Bharat, Persia, and other Eastern countries and was introduced into Greece in c. 524–460 BCE.
